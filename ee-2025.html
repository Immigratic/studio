Key Changes

  • Healthcare ITAs surged by 700%, reflecting a strong focus on healthcare professionals.
  • Program-Based draws dropped by 48.5%, with CEC seeing a 50% reduction.
  • No French-Speaking or Education draws in July, indicating a shift in priorities.

2025 Year-to-Date Key Insights

  • Balanced Distribution: Nearly equal split between Category-Based (24,500) and Program-Based (24,903) ITAs demonstrates Canada's dual-track immigration strategy.
  • Capacity Utilization: Over 50% of 2025 targets achieved by July (63% for Category-Based, 45% for Program-Based), indicating strong pace toward annual goals.
  • French-Speaking Priority: 18,500 ITAs (75% of Category-Based draws) reflect continued emphasis on francophone immigration to support Quebec and bilingual communities.
  • Healthcare Focus: 4,500 healthcare ITAs demonstrate government response to sector shortages, with significant acceleration in Q2/Q3.
  • CEC Dominance: 18,850 CEC ITAs (76% of Program-Based) show preference for candidates with Canadian experience.
  • Annual Projection: On track for 85,000-90,000 total ITAs by year-end, with 38,000-40,000 remaining capacity favoring PNP and healthcare draws.
  • Strategic Timing: Peak activity in February-March (24,862 ITAs) followed by targeted sector-specific draws, indicating planned distribution across quarters.
